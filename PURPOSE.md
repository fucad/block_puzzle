# Free Ad-free Games — Purpose

## The Problem

Mobile gaming has normalized aggressive attention extraction. The simplest games — Snake, Sudoku, Minesweeper, Breakout — are wrapped in unskippable ads, paywalls, and dark patterns that extract value from players without delivering any in return. These games require zero ongoing creative labor, yet players pay with their attention continuously.

## The Belief

We are now in the AI era. A developer with AI assistance can build a polished, app-store-ready casual game in a matter of days. The cost of creating and maintaining simple games has collapsed to nearly zero.

This means there is no longer an economic justification for monetizing them through ads.

Simple entertainment should be a digital public good — like Wikipedia for knowledge, like Linux for infrastructure.

## What We Build

Open-source, ad-free, free-forever casual games. Every game ships to both Android and iOS at no cost to the player, with no advertisements, no paywalls, and no data harvesting. All code is publicly available for anyone to inspect, fork, or contribute to.

## How It Works

- Games are built with Flutter and Flame — an open-source stack, top to bottom.
- All repositories are structured to be AI-friendly, so contributors can understand and improve code with AI assistance.
- Anyone can file issues, submit pull requests, and propose improvements.
- Maintainers review and merge contributions.
- Releases are published to app stores on a regular cadence.

## What This Is Not

This is not an attack on developers or the games industry. Developers deserve to be paid — for building genuinely innovative, complex, and valuable experiences. This movement is specifically about simple arcade and puzzle games that have already been invented, require no ongoing creative labor, and should not be monetized through ad extraction.

If you are building something genuinely new and complex, charge what you want. We are doing something different.

## The Goal

To demonstrate, through working software, that:

- Simple games do not need ads to exist.
- Open-source communities can maintain entertainment sustainably.
- AI makes community-maintained casual gaming viable.
- Casual gaming can be a public good.

The games are proof of concept. The movement is the product.

## Principles

1. **Zero extraction.** No ads, no microtransactions, no data harvesting, no dark patterns.
2. **Radical openness.** All code, all decisions, all contributions are public.
3. **Honest scope.** Simple games only. A game qualifies if its complete mechanics fit in under 500 words.
4. **AI as amplifier.** AI-assisted contribution is embraced and the codebase is structured to support it.
5. **Sustainable minimalism.** No investors, no exit strategy, no overhead beyond what is necessary.
6. **Developer respect.** Developer talent is too valuable to spend on ad-cloning simple games.
